Timeline of Dr. Norman Borlaug's life and accomplishment

  • 1914 - Born in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1933 - Attends the University of Minnesota.
  • 1935 - Has to stop school and save up more money.
  • 1937 - Finishes university and takes a job in the US Forestry Service
  • 1938 - Marries wife of 69 years Margret Gibson. Gets laid off due to budget cuts.
  • 1942 - Receives a Ph.D. in Genetics and Plant Pathology
  • 1944 - Flies to Mexico to head a new plant pathology program.
  • 1945 - Discovers a way to grown wheat twice each season, doubling wheat yields
  • 1962 - Visits Delhi and brings his high-yielding strains of wheat to the Indian.
  • 1970 - receives the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1984 - Becomes a distinguished professor at Texas A&M University
  • 2007 - Receives the congressional gold medal.
  • 2009 - dies at the age of 95.
